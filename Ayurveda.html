What is Ayurveda?

Ayurveda means "the science of life" (ayur means "life" and veda means "science" in Sanskrit). Ayurveda is a discipline of the upaveda or "auxiliary knowledge" in Vedic tradition. Ayurveda has its prime origin from Atharva-Veda and apart as a supplement of the Rig-Veda. Dhanvantari is worshipped as the God of Ayurveda. The aim of this system is to prevent illness, heal the sick, and preserve life. Ayurveda has its origins from India and extended its wings to various parts of the world. Ayurveda was taught in the Gurukula system in ancient days, which has now evolved into undergraduate and postgraduate courses from institutions. Ayurveda is a holistic system of medicine from India that emphasizes balance in the body's systems through diet, herbal treatment, and yogic practices. It focuses on maintaining harmony between the mind, body, and spirit.
